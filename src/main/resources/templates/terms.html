HIPAA Compliance

Protecting your health information rights

1. HIPAA Overview

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) is a federal law that protects the privacy and security of your health information. Axes Hospital is fully committed to HIPAA compliance and protecting your Protected Health Information (PHI).

2. Protected Health Information (PHI)

PHI includes any information about your health status, healthcare provision, or payment that can identify you. This includes:

  • Medical records and test results
  • Billing and insurance information
  • Photos, X-rays, and diagnostic images
  • Prescription and treatment plans
  • Any combination of health data with identifying information

Our HIPAA Commitments

We maintain comprehensive HIPAA compliance through technical safeguards (encryption, access controls), physical safeguards (secure facilities, device security), administrative safeguards (staff training, policies), and regular compliance audits.

3. Your HIPAA Rights

Under HIPAA, you have the right to:

  • Access and obtain copies of your medical records
  • Request amendments to your health information
  • Receive an accounting of disclosures of your PHI
  • Request restrictions on use and disclosure of your PHI
  • Request confidential communications
  • File a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

4. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

We may use and disclose your PHI without authorization for:

  • Treatment, payment, and healthcare operations
  • Public health activities and disease reporting
  • Health oversight activities and audits
  • Legal proceedings and law enforcement purposes
  • Research with proper authorization or waiver
  • Preventing serious threats to health or safety

5. Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a breach of your PHI, we will notify you and relevant authorities in accordance with HIPAA requirements, typically within 60 days of discovering the breach.

Cookie Policy

Understanding how we use cookies

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website. They help us provide a better user experience, remember your preferences, and analyze how you use our system.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the HMS to function properly, including login sessions and security features
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how users interact with the system to improve functionality
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings for a personalized experience
  • Analytics Cookies: Provide statistical information about system usage and performance

3. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set cookies on our behalf, such as analytics providers. These third parties are contractually obligated to use cookies only for the purposes we specify and must comply with our privacy standards.

Cookie Management

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling essential cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of the HMS. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or be notified when new cookies are placed.

4. Cookie Lifespan

We use both session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) and persistent cookies (stored for a specified period). Persistent cookies help us remember your preferences and improve your experience on return visits.

5. Updates to Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology or legal requirements. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
